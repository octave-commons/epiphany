

# @GitHub RIght, there is no reason not to do the fraction of work that is unblocked just waiting for someone to cut it up for you.

Exactly. The policy should not turn unresolved questions into a reason for global waiting. It should require the team to **cut along the uncertainty boundary** and execute the fraction whose contracts and acceptance conditions are already stable.

The real rule is:

> Uncertainty blocks only the work whose scope, contract, safety profile, or acceptance conditions it can materially change—not every related task.

That gives a more useful shape:

```clojure
(-> proposed-shape
    identify-uncertainty-boundaries
    partition-work
    execute-unblocked-slices
    investigate-or-decide-blocked-slices
    Π)
```


## Example

Suppose a JGit integration has two uncertainties:

```text
A. Which exact revision-record schema will persist long-term?
B. How to safely open a local repository and report unavailable/corrupt paths?
```

A should block the persistence adapter and any story that depends on the final durable record contract.

It should **not** block:

- Creating the `extern.git` namespace and foreign-boundary error envelope.
- Writing tests for inaccessible, non-Git, and corrupt-path behavior.
- Defining a minimal observed repository handle contract.
- Establishing a fixture repository and test harness.
- Implementing pure path-byte preservation utilities where their contract is already known.
- Documenting the uncertain schema as an explicit design/open-decision record.

Those are real progress toward Π, not premature implementation.

## Better readiness rule

The current wording should be amended from:

> A story is ready to execute when its remaining questions cannot materially change its declared outcome, contracts, verification, or safety/reversibility profile.

To:

> A story is ready when its **own bounded slice** has stable outcome, applicable contracts, verification, and risk profile. Unresolved questions block only the slices they materially affect. Actors must partition work at those boundaries and may execute every independently verifiable slice whose authority, contract, and acceptance conditions are already sufficient.

And:

> “Needs decomposition” is a planning result, not a holding state. The planner identifies a smallest coherent unblocked slice, records the relation to the blocked work, and moves that slice forward rather than waiting for the entire parent design to become complete.

## What constitutes a good cut

A cut is valid when the child slice can truthfully state:


| Question | Required answer |
| :-- | :-- |
| Outcome | What concrete capability/result will exist after this slice? |
| Boundary | What it deliberately does **not** decide or implement |
| Inputs | Which accepted law/design/ADR constraints it relies on |
| Uncertainty | Which open question exists but cannot invalidate this slice |
| Verification | How its local behavior/contract will be checked |
| Handoff | What evidence and seam the next slice receives |

The work should be split at the *semantic seam*, not merely by file, namespace, or random TODO.

## Design policy amendment

I would add a section immediately after “Design to work”:

```md
### Unblocked-slice rule

Open questions block only the work whose declared outcome, applicable contract,
verification, dependency, or consequence profile they can materially change.
They do not justify holding all work associated with a broader design.

When a material question remains unresolved, the responsible actor partitions the
design into:

- **Unblocked slices:** independently verifiable work whose governing authority,
  boundary, contract, and acceptance conditions are already sufficient.
- **Blocked slices:** work that would require inventing a material contract,
  authority, safety rule, compatibility decision, or verification condition.
- **Resolution work:** the bounded research, design, ADR, experiment, or
  dependency action that can unblock the blocked slice.

A valid unblocked slice states the unresolved question it is insulated from and
the seam it leaves for later work. It must not precommit the answer to that
question by smuggling a provisional choice into a supposedly independent
implementation.

Decomposition is an output of design, not a reason to wait. When no existing
card represents the smallest coherent unblocked slice, create one and relate it
to its blocked sibling/parent.
```

That is the behavior we want agents to follow: **do the lawful fraction now, preserve the seam, and make the blocked fraction explicit.**
