

# @GitHub I believe this work will require an ADR

Yes. This changes the project’s quality architecture: what counts as evidence of correctness, where schemas are enforced, which adapters are trusted, and what CI is allowed to claim. That is durable and cross-cutting enough for an ADR; tool choices and rollout mechanics should live in a companion design document. The repository’s own guidance treats ADRs as architecturally authoritative and asks that each ADR capture a single important, hard-to-reverse decision and its consequences.

## ADR scope

I would make this a **single ADR about the assurance model**, rather than an ADR that commits permanently to Splint, a particular mutation tool, or every CI command.

**Suggested file:** `docs/adrs/adr-004-contract-first-adversarial-verification.md`

**Decision statement:**

> Epiphany will treat schemas, adapter contracts, and independently derived verification evidence as required correctness gates. No single test suite, adapter implementation, metric, or agent-generated report is sufficient evidence of correctness.

That decision captures the enduring policy. The specific implementation—Malli wrappers, clj-kondo, Splint, test.check, Kaocha seeds, a mutation engine, private CI cases—can change without reopening the core architectural decision.
