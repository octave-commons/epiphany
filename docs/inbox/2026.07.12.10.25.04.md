I drafted **Design Practice Policy δ** and attached it. It establishes design as valid proposed-shape data—not a disguised decision, implementation claim, or readiness assertion.

## δ form

The policy uses:

```clojure
(δ design-brief
   research-inputs
   observed-system
   constraints
   alternatives
   open-questions
   -> proposed-shape)
```

`δ` is explicitly pseudocode. It constrains reasoning and future implementation even when the components it names are not yet executable in the repository.

## Design boundary

The policy separates the artifacts that are currently easy to collapse:


| Artifact | Role |
| :-- | :-- |
| Research finding | Bounded evidence-supported claim |
| Design | Proposed shape under stated constraints |
| ADR / decision | Authorized consequential choice |
| Story | Bounded implementation commitment |
| Verification record | Result of a declared check |
| Review record | Explicit evaluation/disposition |

A design can recommend an ADR, ask for research, define a spike, constrain stories, or be superseded. It cannot silently authorize a decision or imply that a proposal has been implemented.

## Kernel integration

The policy locates δ inside your construction kernel, between specification and law/implementation:

```clojure
(η Discovery
   (-> (-> Describe specify
           (δ ... -> proposed-shape)
           define)
       (-> μ shape extern domain infra))
   Π)
```

For material designs, it requires a visible account of the relevant `law`, `shape`, `extern`, `domain`, and `infra` responsibilities. It also permits an explicit “not applicable” statement when a layer genuinely does not apply.

## Story readiness rule

The central operational rule is:

> A story can execute with unresolved questions only when those questions cannot materially change the story’s outcome, contracts, external-boundary behavior, acceptance/verification, or safety/compatibility/reversibility profile.

Otherwise the responsible next action is another design pass, research, an ADR, a bounded spike, or decomposition—not implementation theater.
