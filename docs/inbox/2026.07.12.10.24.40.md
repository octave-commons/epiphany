

# @GitHub yea I added it, document governance. λ

I created **Document Governance Policy λ** and attached it. It treats documents as valid, inspectable data before every template, checker, or workflow fully exists.

## λ form

The policy introduces λ as the document-governance transformation:

```clojure
(λ document-form
   content
   provenance
   relations
   status
   -> governed-artifact)
```

A governed artifact is not automatically correct, authoritative, accepted, or complete. It is an artifact whose kind, claims, provenance, relations, and review state are legible to people and tools.

## What it establishes

The policy now defines:

- A primary document-kind system: notes, research briefs, source records, findings, research reports, designs, decisions, process policies, epics, stories, review records, verification records, examples, anti-examples, generated views, and archives.
- A lifecycle vocabulary: `exploratory`, `draft`, `review`, `active`, `accepted`, `superseded`, `retired`, `archived`, and `rejected`.
- Typed relations such as `sources`, `supports`, `informs`, `implements`, `requires`, `supersedes`, `reviews`, `verifies`, and `accepts`.
- Minimum content contracts for each material kind, while postponing detailed template enforcement until the standards corpus exists.
- A distinction between structural checker findings and human semantic judgment.
- Incremental migration rules: preserve existing evidence first, normalize materially revised documents second, and record unknown legacy shape as a gap rather than fabricating information.
