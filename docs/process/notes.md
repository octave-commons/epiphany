# Notes Practice Policy

## Purpose

Notes are durable, revisable working representations. They preserve context,
observations, extracts, tentative connections, and intermediate synthesis that
is useful before it becomes—or without ever becoming—research, design,
decision, policy, or work.

A note is not failed documentation. It is a distinct artifact kind whose value
is making thought and corpus context inspectable without falsely claiming
authority or completion.

## Note form

```clojure
(ν source-context
   observations
   working-interpretation
   open-questions
   -> revisable-note)
```

A note remains valid data even when incomplete. Its status marks its current
use, not truth.

## Relationship to inbox

Inbox material is raw/near-raw intake; notes are an optional first useful
synthesis. A note preserves source relations to inbox items, exports, artifacts,
or sessions from which it was derived. It may quote or extract only what is
necessary and permitted; it does not replace the source.

Promote a note only by creating a separately governed artifact: a research
finding/report, design, decision candidate, policy proposal, story, source
record, or archive. Do not relabel a note as an accepted artifact merely because
it is old, polished, or often referenced.

## Minimum practice

For a note intended to survive a session, record purpose/context, source links
or origin, direct observations versus interpretations where material, and open
questions/next relation. Retain ambiguity and mark stale, superseded, or
misleading working interpretations rather than silently editing their history
away.

Notes may be freely exploratory. When they become a decision input, public
claim, or implementation authority, apply the appropriate policy and create the
required traceable artifact.
